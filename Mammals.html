Mammals

Platypus

The platypus is a semiaquatic egg-laying mammal that is native to eastern Australia and Tasmania. The platypus is one of five known species of mammals that lay eggs and the platypus senses its prey through electrolocation. Not only is the Platypus one of the only mammals that lay eggs it is also one of the only venomous mammals.

Otter

There are 13 different species of otters which are a part of the mustelidae family. Otters are semiaquatic carnivorous mammals that typically live in cold areas. Otters have extremely high metabolic rates in order to keep themselves warm and must eat 20-25% of their body weight each day to survive depending on the species. Otters are extremely playful animals that engage in various types of different activities and behaviours for their own sheer enjoyment.

Meerkat

Meerkats can be found in various different deserts throughout Africa and are primarily found in large groups know as gangs. The meerkat is a member of the Suricata genus and is a carnivore in the mongoose family. They have a lifespan of around 6-7 years in the wild and weight around 0.5 to 2.5 kilograms. Meerkats have what is known as binocular vision with two eyes on the front of the face.
